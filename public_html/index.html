9 - Flower

A flower is not a part of all types of plant but it is very common. The main purpose of a plant is reproduction, of the individual plant and its species.

Flowers contain a substance called 'pollen' which are the 'male' (microgametophytes) of seeded plants. These are transferred in many different ways, the most common being sticking to bees when they land on the flower. This means the pollen can be transported to other plants that would not be possible otherwise.

8 - Main shoot

The main shoot is the top of the plant where the stem ends and all of the rest of the plant is attached to it.

The most common item to be found at the main shoot is a leaf or a flower. This shoot is still serving the same purpose as the rest of the other shoots but this one tends to be much bigger in size.

7 - Flower buds

Flower buds are undeveloped shoots that normally appear near leaf stalks or the tips of stems.

Once a bud has been formed it may remain for some time in a single condition, or it may shoot immediately. Buds can form into their own plant if it can manage to grow enough on its own.

6 - Leaves

Plants gain energy in a special format called photosynthesis. This process mainly happens in a plants leaves.

The leaves use the light from the Sun, along with carbon dioxide in the Earth's air to make food for the plant it is attached to.

This is why leaves on a plant are large so that they have a bigger surface area to get more energy.

5 - Leaf stalks

Also called petiole, the leaf stalk is responsible for supporting leaves of the plant.

Since leaves are the main component that are responsible for fueling plants through photosynthesis, the leaf stalk serves to transport the energy made in the leaf to the rest of the plant.

4 - The stem

The stem carries water and nutrients gained from the root to all the different parts of the plant.

It is also generally tends to be a sturdy part of a plant providing support and keeping it upright.

3 - Small shoots

Small shoots are small extensions of a plant that mainly grow at the base. When growing around the base of larger plants they are usually called 'suckers'.

The shoot system of a plant works closely with the root system. Shoots get first access of the water and minerals the roots bring in since they are first after the roots.

2 - The main root

The main root is responsible for dividing itself when in the ground to cover as much soil as possible.

This is done to get as much water and nutrients as possible, it is also to provide support for the plant to make sure it stands upright. This protects it against strong winds and other natural causes.

1 - The root

The job of a plants roots is to take up water and other nutrients and minerals from the soil it is sitting inside. Roots spread outwards until it finds a water source.

From this the roots job is to carry the collected water and nutrients to the above portion of the plant.
